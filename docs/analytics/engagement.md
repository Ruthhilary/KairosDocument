# Engagement

Engagement in Kairos measures how actively members are participating in church life — not just whether they show up to services.

## What counts as engagement

Kairos considers a member engaged if they are active across multiple dimensions:

| Activity | Contribution |
|----------|-------------|
| Service attendance | Present, Virtual, or Late in the last 30 days |
| Fellowship participation | Attended a fellowship meeting recently |
| Department activity | Active member of at least one department |
| Follow-up contact | Has been contacted by their leader within the window |

A member who attends service but has no fellowship, no department, and no recent follow-up may still register as low engagement — they are connected to the church but not embedded in community.

## Engagement score on the Dashboard

The **Member Engagement** card in Mission Control shows the current engagement level — **High**, **Medium**, or **Low** — for your branch.

Beneath the score label you'll see:
- Count of **active** members (engaged across dimensions)
- Count of **inactive** members (attended ≤ 38% of services or below threshold)

This is what the **62% active** figure means in the dashboard — not just service attendance but overall member involvement.

## Tracking individual engagement

Leaders and Pastors can see individual engagement signals through:

- **My Attendance** — per-member attendance rate and streak
- **Follow-up notes** — when each member was last contacted and the outcome
- **Department roster** — whether the member is active or stale in the department
- **New Believers pipeline** — whether a new believer is progressing or stalling

## Improving engagement

Common actions that improve engagement scores:

- Log follow-up notes for members who haven't been contacted recently
- Assign stale souls to workers and log contact attempts
- Advance stalled New Believers enrolments
- Ensure fellowship attendance is recorded so participation is visible
