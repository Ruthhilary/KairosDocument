# Church Health

The Church Health view gives Admins and Pastors a single engagement score for their church or branch, backed by the metrics that drive it.

## The engagement score

The overall engagement score appears at the bottom of the Dashboard's Mission Summary section. It reads **High**, **Medium**, or **Low** — calculated from a combination of attendance rate, member follow-up activity, fellowship participation, and new believer progression.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| High | Attendance strong, follow-ups current, pipeline active |
| Medium | Some areas need attention |
| Low | Multiple indicators below threshold — action needed |

## What feeds the score

### Attendance rate

The percentage of active members who attended at least one service in the last 30 days. The target rate is configurable by Admins.

### Member engagement level

Displayed as **High**, **Medium**, or **Low** in the Mission Control dashboard panel. Based on the ratio of attended vs. not-yet-attended members in the current month.

### Follow-up coverage

How many members in the fellowship and department pipelines have been followed up within the configured window. Souls with no follow-up in 7+ days are counted as **Critical** and drag the score down.

### New Believers pipeline

Whether the discipleship pipeline has active enrolments progressing vs. stalling. Stale enrolments (no progress in 7+ days) are surfaced as a warning count on the New Believers page.

## Mission Control Reports panel

The six mini-reports on the Dashboard give you an at-a-glance summary of each contributor to church health:

- **Membership Growth** — member count trend over 6 months
- **Service Attendance** — check-ins over the last 30 days
- **Attendance Rate** — percentage of active members
- **Member Engagement** — High/Medium/Low with active vs. inactive count
- **Attendance by Branch** — comparative bar chart (Admin only)
- **New Believers** — pipeline summary with avg attendance, stale, and completed counts
