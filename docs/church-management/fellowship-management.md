# Fellowship Management

This guide covers how fellowship leaders and admins create, run, and maintain small groups in Kairos.

## Creating a fellowship

Pastors and Admins create fellowships from **Fellowships → + New Fellowship**.

Required fields:
- Name (must be unique within the branch)
- Branch
- Fellowship type (K-Groups, Kharis Express, New Breeds, KOC, KOC Colleges)
- Leader (must be an existing branch member)
- Meeting schedule (e.g. "Every Friday, 6:30 PM")

Optional:
- Co-leader
- Description

## Managing the roster

Once a fellowship exists, members can request to join it. Each member can belong to **one fellowship** at a time.

Fellowship leaders can:
- View the full member roster
- Approve or reject join requests
- Remove a member from the fellowship
- View member contact details

## Logging follow-ups

Follow-up notes on fellowship members help leaders track pastoral care. From the fellowship roster, open a member and add a follow-up:

- Date and time
- Contact method (Phone, WhatsApp, In-Person, etc.)
- Status (Successful, No Answer, etc.)
- Notes

Follow-up notes are visible to the co-leader as well, enabling collaborative pastoral coverage. Leaders receive alerts when a member has not been followed up within the configured window (default: 7 days).

## Recording meeting attendance

From the fellowship detail page:

1. Create a meeting record (date, topic, location)
2. Open the meeting and mark each member's attendance: **Present**, **Absent**, **Excused**, or **Late**
3. Add meeting notes or topics discussed

## Deactivating a fellowship

Pastors and Admins can deactivate a fellowship using the **Deactivate** button on the fellowship card. All meeting records, attendance history, and follow-up notes are preserved.

## Map view

All fellowships are visible on the interactive map. Members can search by postcode or country to find the nearest fellowship to them, with walking/bus/car/train travel times. See [Fellowships](/platform/fellowships) for details.
